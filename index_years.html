The Earth's Geological History

Hadean Eon: The Hadean Eon is the earliest eon in the Earth's history. It began with the formation of the Earth about 4.6 billion years ago and lasted until the end of the Late Heavy Bombardment, which was about 3.8 billion years ago. During this eon, the Earth was being bombarded by asteroids and comets, and there were no continents or oceans.
Archean Eon: The Archean Eon began about 3.8 billion years ago and lasted until about 2.5 billion years ago. During this eon, the Earth's crust and oceans formed, and life appeared on Earth in the form of single-celled organisms.
Proterozoic Eon: The Proterozoic Eon began about 2.5 billion years ago and lasted until about 541 million years ago. This eon is characterized by the appearance of complex multicellular life, including animals and plants. The first glaciers also appeared during this eon.
Paleozoic Era: The Paleozoic Era began about 541 million years ago and lasted until about 252 million years ago. It is divided into six periods: the Cambrian, Ordovician, Silurian, Devonian, Carboniferous, and Permian. During this era, life on Earth evolved rapidly, and many of the major animal groups that exist today first appeared.
Mesozoic Era: The Mesozoic Era began about 252 million years ago and lasted until about 66 million years ago. It is divided into three periods: the Triassic, Jurassic, and Cretaceous. The Mesozoic Era is often referred to as the Age of the Dinosaurs, as these reptiles dominated the Earth during this time.
Cenozoic Era: The Cenozoic Era began about 66 million years ago and continues to the present day. It is divided into two periods: the Paleogene and the Neogene. During this era, mammals evolved and diversified, and humans appeared in the last few million years.
