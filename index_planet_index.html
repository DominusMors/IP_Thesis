A Star is Born

The undetermined journey

Inside a galaxy there are vast areas of dust and gas,tens of light-years in diameter, The formation called Stellar Nebulah

According to Newton's theory of universal gravitation the denser areas of these nebulae tend to become even denser in a way that resembles an avalanche.
The more matter they gather around them, the bigger mass they get and the bigger mass they get, the more they attract matter around them.
This gravitational collapse of a nebula forces matter to very high pressure, speed and temperature.
Thus, in an extremely dense area, a protostar is formed.

Main-Sequence Star

A protostar mainly consists of Hydrogen (H) and Helium (He).
Due to its gravitational collapse, the gravitational potential energy is converted to heat. So when the temperature in the star reaches 1,000,000 °C (18,000,032 °F) thermonuclear fusion of Hydrogen to Helium can take place, producing huge amounts of energy.Every second, the amount of energy produced equals hundreds of billions of nuclear explosions. This procedure lasts for the biggest part of the life of the star and we say in astrophysics that the star is in "the main sequence".
Our sun is in that phase of its life. The size and temperature of a main sequence star are determined by its mass, with more massive stars being hotter and larger than less massive stars. The luminosity of a main sequence star is also proportional to its mass, with more massive stars being more luminous.




White Dwarf

If the star's mass is less than 10 solar masses then its core will never become hot enough for the next thermonuclear reactions to take place. Therefore, it is shrinked and heated while its external parts start to expand again.
At some point, an instability is created between the different parts of the star which results in a complete loss of its outer parts. In its interior, no thermonuclear reactions take place anymore and the pressure required to balance the gravity comes from the electrons that exist in it.
This object is called "White Dwarf" and it will radiate for billions of years losing its energy gradually..
Black Dwarf

Black dwarfs are the final stage in the life of a low to medium mass star.
They are the remnants of white dwarfs, formed due to the gradual cooling and dimming as they burn their remaining fuel. Eventually, they will exhaust their fuel and keep dimming until they are no longer visible to us.
This process takes such a long time that no black dwarfs have formed since the beginning of the Universe, so they are strictly theoretical.




Red Giant / Supergiant

In the case that the mass of the star is bigger than 10 solar masses, at the end of its life, the star becomes a "Red Supergiant", and its core is heated enough for the next cycle of thermonuclear reactions to occur.
Sometimes though, something very impressive happens.
When the time comes for the thermonuclear fusion of iron into heavier elements to happen, a big inversion occurs. Because iron fusion is an endothermic reaction and so it absorbs energy, instead of releasing, when iron fusion is about to start, the core gets cooler instead of getting hotter. The pressure is reduced, and gravity begins to dominate again. The core is compressed and shrinked beyond measure, the pressure of electrons collapses against the vast gravitational power and electrons are forced to merge into protons, creating neutrons.
Now, the core almost exclusively consists of neutrons, touching one another, like a giant atomic nucleus. This stellar core is impossible to contract further. The vast gravitational power, which tries to crash the star, is reflected in this overdense core, leading to a phantasmagorical explosion named "Supernova",ejecting the content of the star to interstellar space in a very high speed.




Supernova Explosion

A supernova is a powerful and luminous explosion of a star. A supernova occurs during the last evolutionary stages of a massive star or when a white dwarf is triggered into runaway nuclear fusion.
The original object, either collapses to a Neutron Star or Black Hole, or is completely destroyed to form a diffuse nebula.
The peak optical luminosity of a supernova can be comparable to that of an entire galaxy before fading over several weeks or months.









Neutron Star / Pulsar

If the core mass is less that two solar masses, then it becomes stable, consists almost exclusively of neutrons and has a very small diameter, around 10 kilometres.
A neutron star is a highly dense celestial object formed from the collapsed core of a massive star that has undergone a supernova explosion. Neutron stars are extremely dense, with a mass similar to that of the Sun but a radius of only about 10 kilometers.
They have extremely strong magnetic fields and rotate rapidly, with some neutron stars completing hundreds of rotations per second. This remainder of a supernova explosion is called "Neutron Star" or "Pulsar".
Neutron stars are highly dense celestial objects formed from the collapsed cores of massive stars, while pulsars are a specific type of neutron star that emit beams of electromagnetic radiation that can be detected as regular pulses
Black hole

If the core mass is bigger than two solar masses, then not even neutrons can restrain this gigantic gravitational power. then not even neutrons can restrain this gigantic gravitational power. This is the absolute dominance of gravity. All mass of the stellar core seems to crash and collapse to an infinitesimal spot which is called "Singularity". Around this space of infinite density an area is created with such a strong gravitational field that not even light can escape from it.
This area is called "Black Hole








Supermassive Black Hole

Supermassive Black Holesare classically defined as black holes with a mass above 100,000 solar masses.
The solar mass (M☉) is a standard unit of mass in astronomy, equal to approximately 2 * 1030 kg. Some Supermassive black holes have masses of several billion solar masses.
Studies have suggested that the maximum natural mass that a black hole can reach, while being luminous accretors (featuring an accretion disk), is typically of the order of about 50 billion M☉ Supermassive black holes,are much larger and more massive than their stellar counterparts. They are typically found at the centers of galaxies and can have masses ranging from millions to billions of times that of the Sun. It is currently believed that supermassive black holes are formed through the merging of smaller black holes or through the direct collapse of large amounts of gas and dust in the early universe.
In addition to their size and mass, supermassive black holes also have a much greater impact on the structure and evolution of galaxies than regular black holes. They can influence the motion of stars and gas in their vicinity and play a key role in shaping the overall structure of the galaxy.
Some supermassive black holes are also observed to be actively accreting matter from their surroundings, producing intense radiation and powerful jets of particles that can be observed across vast distances.
